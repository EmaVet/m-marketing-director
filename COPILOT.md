# Marketing Copilot adoption across APAC

The JD asks for someone who can drive "product trial, activation, and ongoing usage." In 2026, at GitHub, that is largely a Copilot problem.

This is how I think about it.

---

## The adoption challenge is not awareness

Developers know Copilot exists. The challenge is getting from "I've heard of it" to "I use it every day" to "my whole team is on it and we can't work without it."

That is a different marketing problem to most. It is not a demand generation problem. It is an activation and education problem — and in APAC, it plays out differently across every market.

---

## Three audiences, three jobs

**Developers** need to feel it, not hear about it. The only thing that converts a developer is a hands-on experience where Copilot does something useful in their actual workflow. Workshops, community events, user groups. The JD calls this out specifically. It is the right instinct. You cannot talk a developer into adoption — you have to give them a reason to stay.

**Technical decision makers** need to see team-level impact. Time saved, code quality, onboarding speed. The story shifts from individual productivity to team capability. This is where case studies, ROI frameworks, and peer community come in.

**Executive buyers** need commercial justification. Headcount efficiency, developer retention, speed to ship. This audience does not care about the product — they care about the outcome. The marketing job is translating developer love into business language without losing either audience in the process.

---

## The APAC nuance

There is no single APAC market. Enterprise buying behaviour in Japan is not the same as in Singapore, and neither resembles India or ANZ. Localisation here is not translation — it is understanding how trust gets built in each context, who the real decision maker is, and what proof points carry weight.

A developer community event that works in Sydney needs a different approach in Tokyo. An enterprise case study that lands in ANZ may need reframing entirely for SEA.

---

## What I would build

Programs that start with the developer and work outward. Hands-on activation events that create genuine product usage, not attendance metrics. Content that helps technical buyers make the internal case. Regional community infrastructure that sustains itself beyond the campaign.

The measure is not pipeline created. It is product usage that holds.
